Climate Adaptation & Maternal Health: A Cost-Effectiveness Analysis

Climate & Health Cost-Effectiveness Analysis Maternal Health R Shiny Dashboard

Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH), excessive bleeding after childbirth, is a leading cause of maternal death in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). Rising global temperatures worsen this: heat degrades the standard preventive drug (oxytocin), making it less effective in hot climates. This project builds a cost-effectiveness model comparing oxytocin against heat-stable carbetocin, a newer alternative that requires no refrigeration.

Using CMIP6 climate projections and population data across 2020–2059, the model estimates lives saved and cost per death averted across world regions. Results are presented in an interactive Shiny dashboard with real-time filtering by region and year.

Pulmonary Embolism Incidence Patterns: PE-EHR+ Study

Health Informatics Electronic Health Records Python Epidemiology

Pulmonary embolism (PE), a blood clot in the lungs, is life-threatening but often preventable. Despite its severity, patterns of PE diagnosis and care complexity are not well-characterized across diverse patient populations. This project uses electronic health record (EHR) data from the PE-EHR+ dataset to analyze how PE is diagnosed, managed, and documented across hospitals and patient groups.

Working in Python, the analysis examines trends in PE incidence, differences across demographic groups, and the complexity of clinical care pathways, surfacing insights that can improve hospital protocols and reduce preventable mortality.

SARS-CoV-2 Variant Dominance Analysis (2022–2024)

Epidemiology Infectious Disease R Data Visualization Reproducible Research

As the COVID-19 pandemic evolved, successive waves were driven by different SARS-CoV-2 variants, each with distinct transmission rates and clinical severity. Understanding which variants dominated, and when, is essential for evaluating public health responses and preparing for future outbreaks.

This project analyzes U.S. surveillance data from December 2022 through October 2024, tracking variants that achieved greater than 5% national prevalence. Using fully reproducible R code, the analysis produces clear visualizations of variant succession to inform vaccine strategy and outbreak preparedness planning.

Academic & Coursework Projects

Blood Lead Levels & Hypertension in U.S. Adults

Epidemiology NHANES R Environmental Health

Lead exposure remains a significant but underappreciated public health threat in the United States. This project used the NHANES 2015–16 dataset to examine the association between blood lead levels and hypertension in U.S. adults, applying advanced quantitative methods with careful attention to confounding factors and mediation pathways in R. The analysis contributes to evidence on how environmental toxin exposure shapes cardiovascular disease risk across populations.

Nurture First: Breastfeeding & Complementary Feeding in Rural Vietnam

Nutrition Program Design Global Health UNICEF

Inadequate infant feeding practices contribute to child malnutrition and stunting globally. This project designed a comprehensive nutrition program implementation plan for rural communities in Vietnam, grounded in UNICEF formative research. The plan addressed maternal nutrition knowledge, breastfeeding support, and complementary feeding practices, demonstrating applied evidence-based intervention design and program management skills.

Healthy Heart Program Evaluation: School-Based NCD Prevention, Argentina

Program Evaluation M&E NCD Prevention Adolescent Health

Non-communicable diseases like heart disease and diabetes are increasingly affecting young people globally. This project designed a monitoring and evaluation (M&E) framework for a school-based nutrition and physical activity intervention in Argentina targeting adolescent NCD prevention. The framework assessed program reach, implementation fidelity, and health outcomes using evidence-based M&E methods including RE-AIM.

HPV Vaccination Policy Brief — Bihar, India

Health Policy Immunization Health Equity India

HPV vaccination can prevent the majority of cervical cancer cases, yet coverage remains uneven across populations and geographies. This evidence-based policy brief analyzed HPV vaccination coverage gaps and equity considerations in Bihar, India, one of the country's most underserved states, and proposed actionable recommendations for improving immunization uptake among priority populations.

Carbon Reduction Challenge — Georgia Tech Initiative

Climate Sustainability ESG Interdisciplinary

In a cross-disciplinary collaboration with Georgia Tech engineering students and Energy Efficient Technologies (EET), this project analyzed HVAC oil fouling, a common and costly source of energy waste in large buildings. The team designed an efficiency intervention projected to save $39M annually, cut carbon emissions by 18,000 tons, and reduce energy consumption by 10M+ kWh per year, directly aligned with corporate ESG and sustainability reporting goals.
